Controls &
SOX Support.

Internal controls and SOX/ICFR support — for US-listed obligations and for UK/Ireland Provision 29 readiness.

Controls work is where my background runs deepest. Six years across PwC and Deloitte on SOX, ICFR, SOC and ISAE engagements — designing risk and control matrices, running walkthroughs and testing, identifying deficiencies and supporting remediation, and coordinating with external auditors. Whether your client answers to a US-listed parent or is preparing for the UK's new internal-controls declaration, the underlying discipline — COSO-based, evidence-driven controls testing — is the same, and it's what I do.

  • check_circle Design and operating-effectiveness testing through structured walkthroughs and evidence-based sampling
  • check_circle Coordination with external auditors on scope, evidence and timing
  • check_circle SOC and ISAE 3402 third-party assurance readiness
UK & Ireland

Provision 29

For UK-listed organisations preparing for Provision 29 of the revised UK Corporate Governance Code 2024. Applies to accounting periods beginning on or after 1 January 2026.

  • check_circle Mapping and documenting material controls
  • check_circle Designing evidence and monitoring needed to support a board declaration
  • check_circle Control testing, gap identification and remediation planning

Provision 29 is principles-based and comply-or-explain — broader than US SOX, and not externally audited in the same way. This support is framed as readiness and uplift, not an audit opinion. The COSO framework underpins both regimes, so the controls discipline carries directly across.
