Controls & SOX Support

Internal controls and SOX/ICFR support — for US-listed obligations and for UK/Ireland Provision 29 readiness.

Controls work is where my background runs deepest. Six years across PwC and Deloitte on SOX, ICFR, SOC and ISAE engagements — designing risk and control matrices, running walkthroughs and testing, identifying deficiencies and supporting remediation, and coordinating with external auditors. Whether your client answers to a US-listed parent or is preparing for the UK's new internal-controls declaration, the underlying discipline — COSO-based, evidence-driven controls testing — is the same, and it's what I do.

US SOX / ICFR

SOX 404 & ICFR Support

For businesses with US-listed parents, group reporting obligations, or operations in scope of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act.

  • Risk and control matrix (RCM) design — entity-level, IT general, and business-process controls
  • Design and operating-effectiveness testing through structured walkthroughs and evidence-based sampling
  • Deficiency identification, root cause analysis and remediation support
  • Workpaper preparation and review
  • Coordination with external auditors on scope, evidence and timing
  • SOC and ISAE 3402 third-party assurance readiness

Built on direct PwC and Deloitte experience across global insurance, banking and industrial clients.

UK & Ireland

Provision 29 & Internal Controls Readiness

For UK-listed organisations preparing for Provision 29 of the revised UK Corporate Governance Code 2024 — the requirement for boards to declare annually on the effectiveness of their risk management and internal control framework.

Applies to accounting periods beginning on or after 1 January 2026. Covers financial, operational, reporting and compliance controls.

  • Mapping and documenting material controls
  • Designing the evidence and monitoring needed to support a board declaration
  • Control testing and gap identification
  • Remediation planning

Provision 29 is principles-based and comply-or-explain — broader than US SOX, and not externally audited in the same way. This support is framed as readiness and uplift, not an audit opinion. The COSO framework underpins both regimes, so the controls discipline carries directly across.
