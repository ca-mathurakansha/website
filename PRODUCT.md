# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

UK and Irish accounting practices (sole practitioners to small firms) and SMEs who need qualified remote accounting support. They arrive evaluating a candidate for ongoing or project-based work — bookkeeping, VAT, year-end, management reporting, or controls/SOX readiness. Their primary concern is professional credibility: they want to know this is a qualified CA with Big 4 background, not an unqualified bookkeeper or a volume outsourcing service.

Secondary audience: UK/Irish SMEs who don't work through an accountant but want their own books handled by someone qualified.

## Product Purpose

A trust-first marketing site that converts professional service seekers into consulting inquiries. Akansha Mathur is dual-qualified (ICAI India + ACA Chartered Accountants Ireland), 6 years Big 4 (PwC, Deloitte), remote from India. The site's job is to make that background feel immediately legible and credible — so a UK practice principal who lands here says "yes, this is the right person" and makes contact.

Success = qualified inbound inquiries. Not volume; quality.

## Brand Personality

Sharp · Professional · Warm

Technically rigorous but personable. The voice of a senior practitioner you'd want to call — not a faceless offshore service, not a stuffy institutional firm, not a startup. Expert confidence delivered without distance.

## Anti-references

- **Generic accounting SaaS** (Xero, QuickBooks, FreeAgent style) — teal palette, friendly feature grids, consumer-facing. This site is for professionals, not end-users.
- **Overcrowded freelancer portfolio** (Fiverr/Upwork style) — cluttered social proof badges, pricing tiers, "hire me" desperation. No race-to-the-bottom positioning.
- **Stuffy law-firm website** — dark wood panelling, Latin mottos, impersonal institutional authority. Structure and rigour, yes; warmth deliberately present.
- **Indian BPO / outsourcing site** — price-led, offshore-generic, volume-focused ("500 clients served"). This is a named practitioner with a specific pedigree, not a commodity service.

## Design Principles

1. **Credentials first, price never** — The Big 4 background and dual qualification are the entire differentiation. Lead with them everywhere. Never position on cost, even though cost is a real advantage; let the quality imply the value.
2. **Make the distance disappear** — Remote India-based supporting UK/Irish clients. The site must never feel offshore. Timezone awareness is a feature, not an apology.
3. **Editorial restraint** — Every element earns its place. Silence between elements is as intentional as the content itself. Nothing decorative that doesn't serve the message.
4. **Direct over persuasive** — No superlatives, no buzzwords, no vague claims. Clear scope, honest limitations (work is always returned for the client's review and filing). Trust is built through specificity.
5. **Warmth within structure** — The grid, hairlines, and serif typography create professional structure. The copy, spacing, and personal tone create warmth. Neither collapses into the other.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Primary concerns: colour contrast (navy on parchment is fine; amber on white needs checking), keyboard navigation, focus states visible on all interactive elements, skip-to-content link present.
